Management Approach

Terrell Hull holds several industry certifications in project management, has extensive management experience, and has been held accountable for budget and profitability for numerous large-scale software projects, ranging from a dozen to over 50 direct reports. His practical management experience is complimented with a solid formal educational background in business management, project management, managerial accounting, and advanced psychology coursework in motivational leadership.

A "Theory Y" manager, he believes that the key to managing well is leadership. Leadership requires a genuine concern for each member of the team, and a leader is careful to listen to team members. Team members must feel as though they are part of the organization. Participative management and empowerment are very effective means of eliciting the Hawthorne Effect, which he has found to be a powerful tool in leading people to achieve above and beyond expectations. Terrell has a special gift for motivating teams, and is a dedicated mentor and example to his team. He is very effective at inspiring team members, so that they look forward to coming to work. He gives purpose and meaning to the work, and impresses upon them the value of their contributions to the overall team achievement. He has found that when team members enjoy what they are doing, and feel that they have the training and tools to do their jobs effectively, they tend to perform best.

He subscribes to the "Two-factor Theory of Motivation," which holds that hygiene and motivation are inextricably linked to employee productivity and job satisfaction. Hygiene encompasses the following factors:

  • Working conditions
  • Tools
  • Socio-political environment
  • Corporate culture
  • Interpersonal relationships
  • Compensation
  • Job security
  • Company policies and administration

Motivation is comprised of the potential opportunities for:

  • Achievement
  • Increased responsibility
  • Recognition
  • Growth

Using this approach, Terrell has been particularly effective at bringing out the best in teams and individuals, and helping them achieve more than they thought possible. Terrell has received numerous commendations for his natural abilities as a leader, and works constantly to increase his knowledge and experience in management. He enjoys management, because it provides him with an opportunity to learn more about himself, and how to be a better leader and mentor to his teams.
